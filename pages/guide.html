Above can be seen the basic connection between an electric motor and the drive axle. The sprocket and motor are labelled to demonstrate which count of teeth should be used in the calculations. Having more teeth on the axle and less on the motor will result in a greater torque but lower top speed and vice versa. It should also be noted that a sprocket with fewer teeth will be subject to more ware than one with more teeth.

In this example, the axle sprocket has 55 teeth and the motor sprocket has 11 teeth, giving a gearing ratio of 1/5 reduction.

Here is the wider picture for the same go-kart. I should be noted that the only one of the rear wheels drives the kart in this single-motor layout, to provide differential and improve cornering
